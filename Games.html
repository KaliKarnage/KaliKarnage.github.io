University Projects

360 Video Fishing Game

This portfolio artifact highlights a collaborative endeavor undertaken in my sophomore year at university, where we were tasked with developing an immersive interactive video. Utilizing 360-degree video technology and the Unity engine, we crafted an experience designed to run seamlessly on both the Oculus Quest 2 and Quest Pro. My primary contributions to the project included the creation of 3D models, texturing, and designing the User Interface (UI). For model creation, I employed Autodesk Maya, while Substance Painter was my choice for texturing. This project marked my inaugural venture into using both Maya and Substance Painter. I eagerly embraced these tasks as an opportunity to challenge myself, aiming to master these sophisticated tools and enhance my proficiency in 3D modeling and texturing. This experience was not only a significant leap in my technical skillset but also a testament to the power of collaborative innovation in creating compelling virtual reality experiences.

Individual Immersive Game (Enigma)

This portfolio artifact showcases a solo game development project I undertook during my tenure in the Games, Interactive Media, and Mobile (GIMM) program at Boise State University. The assignment challenged me to design and create a fully immersive Virtual Reality (VR) game for the Oculus Quest 2, utilizing exclusively original assets. The project's inception occurred in my sophomore year, with the preparation of comprehensive Game Design Documents. Subsequently, the actual game development commenced at the onset of my junior year and concluded at the end of my senior year. This endeavor not only allowed me to hone my technical and creative skills but also to contribute a distinctive VR experience to the gaming community.

Mobile Data Narrative

This portfolio piece reflects an individual assignment designed to deepen my understanding of web development technologies, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, and Node.js. The objective was to construct a responsive website that could dynamically gather and display data. Leveraging D3, I was tasked with creating three insightful graphs to narrate a compelling story. I chose to focus on the evolution of weapons in the popular MMORPG, World of Warcraft, tracking their progression from the game's original launch in 2004 through to the Legion expansion. This project not only showcased my technical prowess in web development and data visualization but also allowed me to explore the fascinating lore and intricate details behind one of the most iconic video games.
