What is NASA SUITS

The NASA SUITS (Space Suit User Interface Technologies for Students) program is an initiative designed to engage and inspire students by giving them the opportunity to design and create spacesuit information displays within an augmented reality (AR) environment. This program is part of NASA's efforts to foster innovation and technological development in the field of space exploration, specifically focusing on the enhancement of astronaut spacesuits and their interfaces.

Students participating in the NASA SUITS program are tasked with developing AR solutions that can be integrated into the helmets of astronauts' spacesuits. These solutions aim to improve astronauts' efficiency and safety by providing them with critical information through the helmet's display. This could include navigation data, health and life support system statuses, and other environmental readings crucial for spacewalks and other extravehicular activities.

The program is structured as a challenge, inviting teams from various universities and colleges to submit their designs for evaluation. Selected teams are then invited to NASA's Johnson Space Center to test their AR interfaces in simulated space environments, offering a hands-on experience that closely mirrors real-world space exploration scenarios. This not only allows students to contribute directly to NASA's space exploration efforts but also provides them with invaluable experience in teamwork, problem-solving, and the application of AR technology in demanding environments.

NASA SUITS serves as a unique educational outreach initiative, promoting STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) education and encouraging the next generation of scientists, engineers, and explorers to contribute to the future of space exploration.
