What is NASA SUITS

The NASA SUITS (Space Suit User Interface Technologies for Students) program is an initiative designed to engage and inspire students by giving them the opportunity to design and create spacesuit information displays within an augmented reality (AR) environment. This program is part of NASA's efforts to foster innovation and technological development in the field of space exploration, specifically focusing on the enhancement of astronaut spacesuits and their interfaces.

Students participating in the NASA SUITS program are tasked with developing AR solutions that can be integrated into the helmets of astronauts' spacesuits. These solutions aim to improve astronauts' efficiency and safety by providing them with critical information through the helmet's display. This could include navigation data, health and life support system statuses, and other environmental readings crucial for spacewalks and other extravehicular activities.

The program is structured as a challenge, inviting teams from various universities and colleges to submit their designs for evaluation. Selected teams are then invited to NASA's Johnson Space Center to test their AR interfaces in simulated space environments, offering a hands-on experience that closely mirrors real-world space exploration scenarios. This not only allows students to contribute directly to NASA's space exploration efforts but also provides them with invaluable experience in teamwork, problem-solving, and the application of AR technology in demanding environments.

NASA SUITS serves as a unique educational outreach initiative, promoting STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) education and encouraging the next generation of scientists, engineers, and explorers to contribute to the future of space exploration.

My NASA SUITS Journey

In my role as the UI (User Interface) team lead for the Boise State NASA SUITS team during the 2022-2023 school year, I was tasked with spearheading efforts to develop augmented reality interfaces for astronaut spacesuits. This role was particularly challenging and enriching as it was my first time working with MRTK (Mixed Reality Toolkit), Meta Quest 2, and HoloLens 2. Here's a detailed look at how I approached my responsibilities, with an emphasis on adapting to and learning these new technologies:

  • Leadership and Team Coordination: Leading the team, I was responsible for guiding our members through the development process, setting clear goals, and ensuring that we collaboratively worked towards crafting effective AR solutions. This leadership role also involved fostering a learning environment where we could collectively explore the capabilities of our new tools.

  • Project Management: Managing the UI project segment required careful planning and execution, especially considering the learning curve associated with the new technologies we were using. I had to ensure that our project met NASA's requirements and stayed aligned with the overall goals of the NASA SUITS Challenge, all while navigating the complexities of Unity, MRTK, Meta Quest 2, and HoloLens 2.

  • Design and Development:My direct involvement in the design and development process was crucial. Using Unity as our foundation, I led the team in learning how to integrate MRTK to enhance our designs for better usability within the AR space. The development process was a continuous cycle of prototyping, testing, and refining our work, especially as we became more familiar with the nuances of developing for Meta Quest 2 and HoloLens 2.

  • Technical Expertise and Problem Solving:Facing the challenge of my first time working with MRTK, Meta Quest 2, and HoloLens 2, I had to adapt quickly and efficiently. I immersed myself in learning these technologies, leveraging online resources, tutorials, and community forums to accelerate my understanding. This rapid learning process was essential for troubleshooting technical challenges and innovating within our project's scope. My ability to quickly grasp these technologies enabled me to lead my team effectively, ensuring our UI designs were feasible and optimized for our target platforms.

  • Communication and Collaboration: Clear and effective communication was key to our success, especially as we navigated the steep learning curve of our chosen technologies. I made it a priority to facilitate open discussions about our learning experiences, challenges, and solutions. Collaborating closely with other sub-teams, I ensured that our AR interfaces seamlessly integrated with other aspects of the spacesuit design, fostering a holistic development approach.

  • Documentation and Reporting:Throughout the project, I documented our learning process, design decisions, and the evolution of our understanding of MRTK, Meta Quest 2, and HoloLens 2. This documentation was not only crucial for reporting our progress to NASA and participating in reviews but also served as a valuable knowledge base for the team and future projects.

Leading the UI team in the NASA SUITS Challenge as a novice in MRTK, Meta Quest 2, and HoloLens 2 was a formidable yet rewarding experience. It pushed me to quickly adapt and learn, enhancing my technical skills and leadership capabilities, and ultimately contributing to the development of innovative AR interfaces for space exploration.

Astronaut Steve Swanson speaking to students for an event at Maple Grove Elemetary School

An outreach event held by our Boise State University NASA SUITS team at Maple Grove Elementary School, I, along with other team members, including the esteemed Steve Swanson, had the incredible opportunity to introduce a solar system simulator through the Meta Quest 2s to the students. Our collective aim was to immerse these young learners in the vast wonders of our solar system, allowing them to explore planets, moons, and stars in a way that textbooks simply cannot convey. Having Steve Swanson with us added an invaluable layer of insight and experience, as we shared our passion for space exploration and science with the students.

The enthusiasm and curiosity from the students were palpable as they donned the Meta Quest 2s and embarked on their virtual space journeys. It was a privilege to guide them through this experience, answer their insightful questions, and see their young minds light up with fascination. This event wasn't just about showcasing the capabilities of augmented reality in education; it was a powerful reminder of how teamwork and technology can come together to inspire the next generation of scientists, engineers, and explorers. Witnessing the impact of our outreach firsthand, alongside my team and Steve Swanson, was truly inspiring and a moment I'll cherish.
